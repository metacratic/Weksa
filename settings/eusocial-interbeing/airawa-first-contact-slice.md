# Airawa First-Contact Slice

This slice records the room consensus from the first Airawa introduction
conversation. It is not a full language project yet. It is a durable pressure
note for the first hand-authored Airawa language-project seed.

## Consensus

Airawa should sound strong and imposing without becoming decorative noise. The
sound should feel deliberate, clipped, and socially heavy: hard stops, closed
syllables, dorsal/coronal pressure, and forms that can land like claims rather
than flow like scenery.

The first vocabulary should not begin with random nature nouns. Airawa's first
usable words should live where Airawa society puts pressure on speech:

- identity and standing
- formal naming
- hearth or origin affiliation
- approach permission
- border-right and access
- peace claim
- obligation or sworn bond
- gratitude as acknowledged debt
- memory-line or lineage record
- witness, authority, guest, guard, warrior, hunter, elder
- mother-tree access, nutrient gift, surplus, taboo, and delivery through an
  authorized intermediary

The language should enter use through social mechanics: who may approach, who
may answer, what cannot be asked directly, what must be witnessed, and what
obligation is created by accepting a gift.

## Translation Boundary

The proposed chieftain/visitor scene is a good first test case, but it should
not be translated by substituting English words one by one. A full Airawa
rendering is blocked until the project owns at least:

- a starter lexicon for rank, kinship, place, trade, gift, access, and danger;
- a permission model for outsider, host, witness, elder, guard, warrior, and
  mother-tree access;
- grammar for questions, offers, refusals, permission, obligation, honor, and
  temporal appointment;
- a way to distinguish direct speech to a mother-tree from authorized delivery
  to it;
- a rule for whether scars are bodily evidence, honor claims, debt markers, or
  warrior-status signals.

Until those are owned, Weksa may produce provisional glosses or placeholder
packets, but not claim a clean full Airawa translation.

Useful sample frames, still provisional:

- `Atar qatar Serak.` -- "I stand as formally named Serak."
- `Atar daruq ...` -- "I stand from hearth-origin ..."
- `Tazek karuq?` -- "May I approach / claim approach-right?"
- `Kazhar.` -- "I come in peace."
- `Sakar vezhar.` -- "You are guest under peace."

## First Scene Target

The first Airawa scene should be a narrow dialogue with speaker locks:

- host or chieftain asks the outsider's purpose;
- outsider gives name, origin, status, peaceful intent, and nutrient-gift offer;
- host refuses direct mother-tree access but accepts intermediary delivery;
- visitor acknowledges gratitude/debt;
- host marks witnessed warrior promise and offers training;
- visitor accepts honor and names the scar as evidence;
- host appoints a sunrise return.

This scene is valuable because it forces the language to decide what owns
access, obligation, witness, honor, and bodily proof. That is the machine worth
building. The word list is only the ash it leaves on the table.
