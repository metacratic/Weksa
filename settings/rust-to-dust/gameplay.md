# Gameplay Notes

Rust to Dust can use spectacle, combat, and resistance fantasy, but its ending
should not fake seriousness with a ceremonial single-button prompt.

If a scene is fundamentally cinematic, let it be a cutscene. Bad interactivity
is worse than no interactivity because it asks the player to perform meaning
without giving them agency. "Press a key to solemnly acknowledge theme" is not a
system. It is UI wearing a little funeral hat and hoping nobody notices.

## Ending Delivery

The pub ending beat can remain a scene:

- the alien TV is still beautiful and seductive
- the room is no longer organized around it
- the father is present but not central
- his son is there, changed but choosing
- someone turns the TV down because another person is about to sing

That does not need a button prompt.

## If The Ending Is Playable

If the ending becomes gameplay, it should use sustained verbs and systems that
have already earned their meaning.

Possible playable shapes:

- participating in the distributed commons over a full sequence
- helping move practices, songs, tools, archives, and skills through a local
  network
- making tradeoffs that preserve relationships rather than trophies
- coordinating care, memory, repair, and mutual aid under pressure
- choosing how to keep something alive without making Dad central again

The gameplay should express local alignment, cooperation, and durable practice.
It should not reduce the thesis to a quicktime event, a single symbolic prompt,
or a final checklist of ideology chores.
