# Rust to Dust Setting

Rust to Dust is about cultural absorption by a more powerful empire.

The aliens control a galaxy-spanning network. They do not need to defeat
humanity through ordinary conquest. They set up shop, connect Earth to imperial
markets and media, and make alien status, technology, style, and opportunity too
valuable to ignore.

The result is not instant occupation. It is generational replacement.

Human children grow up fluent in alien memes and prestige codes. Alien aesthetic
norms enter daily life. Human cultural inheritance becomes an asset class,
fashion feedstock, collectible nostalgia, and diplomatic currency. The old world
does not vanish in one dramatic fire. It gets priced, packaged, licensed, and
drained.

## Core Tension

The setting should make dominant-culture audiences feel the asymmetry they often
export elsewhere: the humiliation of watching their heritage become local color
inside somebody else's market.

## Empire Pattern

- The empire frames absorption as access, uplift, taste, and opportunity.
- Military conquest is unnecessary when economic and cultural gravity can do the
  work.
- Human resistance can be dangerous locally and still become amusing imperial
  content.
- Human authenticity is valuable because it is raw, provincial, and marketable.
- The empire does not need humans to love it. It needs human life to route
  through imperial systems.

## Open Naming

The aliens and their language are not named yet. Names should preferably emerge
from the first language project's ontology and phonetics rather than from
English-facing branding.
